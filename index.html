Chapter One

What is Nuviá?

Nuviá is a constructed language built so that English speakers can learn it quickly while sounding distinctly European. Its name comes from nuovo (new) + via (way): the new way.

LanguageContribution to Nuviá
EnglishVocabulary roots and sentence structure
ItalianVowel sounds, articles, gender endings, -zióne forms
GermanThe letter ß — long or emphasized S sound
FrenchMelodic speech rhythm; accent marks for stress only
Three Core Principles

Predictability — pronunciation always follows spelling.
Pronounceability — every written letter is spoken.
Shared learning — vocabulary grows by applying simple rules to English words.

First Words in Nuviá

Salé! Como tu stai?Hello! How are you?
Sofia
Iu stai bene, grátie! E tu?I'm fine, thank you! And you?
Marco
Stai bene anche. Grátie!Also fine, thanks! (pronoun "Iu" dropped — "-ai" makes context clear)

Chapter Two

Pronunciation Rules

Nuviá targets Italian vowel clarity with French melodic rhythm. Every written letter is spoken — no silent letters. Vowels are always pure, never reduced.

The Golden Rule

Stress falls on the second-to-last syllable by default. Accent marks only override this — they never change vowel quality.

nu·VÍ·a  ·  par·LÁ·mos  ·  e·du·ca·ZIÓ·ne

Chapter Eight

Verbs & Conjugation

Nuviá verbs are fully regular — no exceptions. Infinitives end in -are (primary class) or -ere (secondary). Conjugate by removing the ending and adding the suffix for each person.

💡

Pronoun Drop Rule: The pronouns Iu and Tu are optional. Because every verb ending in Nuviá is unique, the conjugation alone identifies the subject — just like in Spanish or Italian. Parlo Nuviá is identical in meaning to Iu parlo Nuviá; the -o ending already tells you it's first person. Likewise, Parlas bene! clearly means "you speak well!" without needing Tu. The other pronouns (Il, Ila, Nu, Voi, Ilos, Ilas) are generally kept since they carry more information.

TuYou (singular)Optional — -as ending already signals 2nd person

Future Tense — va + infinitive

Analytic Future

Place va before the infinitive. The main verb stays unchanged.

  • Va parlareI will speak / You will speak (pronoun optional)
  • Ila va amareShe will love
  • Ilos va videreThey will see

Chapter Ten

Asking Questions

Nuviá has two methods for yes/no questions and a set of question words for open-ended ones.

Method 1 — Intonation

Normal word order; raise pitch at end.

Parlas Nuviá?
Ila ama il car?
"Do you speak?" / "Does she love the car?"

Method 2 — Particle ke

Place ke at start (more formal).

Ke parlas Nuviá?
Ke ila ama il car?
More emphatic or formal form

Question Words

kewhat
kiuwho
ondewhere
kandewhen
por kewhy
komohow
Examples
  • Ke est il fone?What is the phone?
  • Kiu parlas?Who speaks? ("Tu" dropped)
  • Onde andas?Where are you going? ("Tu" dropped)
  • Kande nu va parlare?When will we speak?
  • Por ke no parlas?Why don't you speak?

Chapter Eleven

Negation & Emphasis

Place no directly before the verb to negate any sentence. Multiple negatives are permitted for emphasis.

Basic Pattern

SUBJECT  +  NO  +  VERB

  • No parloI do not speak ("Iu" dropped)
  • Ila no amaShe does not love
  • Nu no andamosWe are not going
Double Negation for Emphasis
  • No parlo no cosaI speak absolutely nothing
  • No parla no!Don't speak at all!
Across All Tenses
  • No parlo NuviáI don't speak (present)
  • No parleo NuviáI didn't speak (past)
  • No va parlare NuviáI won't speak (future)
  • No parla!Don't speak! (imperative)
