Chapter Two

Prepositions & Location

Prepositions connect nouns to the rest of a sentence, expressing location, direction, time, and relationship. In Nuviá they are short, invariable words that precede the noun they govern.

NuviáEnglishExample
deof / fromil libro de la vida — the book of life
ato / at / towardAndo a la casa — I'm going to the house
enin / insideEst en il parko — She is in the park
conwithParlo con amicos — I speak with friends
sinwithoutCafe sin lecho — coffee without milk
porfor / because of / throughEsto aquí por tu — I'm here because of you
antebefore (position / time)Ante la casa — in front of the house
posafter (position / time)Pos il ceno — after dinner
entrebetweenEntre il mar e il ciel — between the sea and the sky
sobreon / above / aboutIl libro sobre la tabla — the book on the table
bajounder / belowBajo il ponte — under the bridge
Contractions with il

When de or a immediately precede the masculine article il, they contract:

  • de + il → deldel libro — of the book
  • a + il → alal parko — to the park

These contractions do not apply to the feminine article la: de la casa stays de la casa.

Using Prepositions
Kai
Onde andas pos il ceno?Where are you going after dinner?
Luna
Ando al parko con Sofia. Veni con nos!I'm going to the park with Sofia. Come with us!
Kai
Vale! Ma parlo con Marco ante.Okay! But I'll speak with Marco first (before).

  3. Ila anda al parko pos la escola

Chapter Three

Conjunctions & Complex Sentences

Conjunctions link clauses and ideas together. With them, you can express conditions, reasons, contrasts, and sequences — moving from simple statements into fully formed thoughts.

NuviáEnglishExample
eandParlo e escucho — I speak and listen
oorTe o cafe? — Tea or coffee?
mabutVolo andare, ma no posso — I want to go but I can't
porquebecauseParlo porque amo Nuviá — I speak because I love Nuviá
quandowhenQuando parlas, escucho — When you speak, I listen
siifSi parlas, entiende — If you speak, (he/she) understands
aunquealthough / even thoughAunque est tarde, parlo — Although it's late, I speak
mientraswhileMangio mientras parlas — I eat while you speak
antes kebefore (conjunction)Parla antes ke andas — Speak before you go
pos keafter (conjunction)Pos ke mangeamos, andamos — After we eat, we go
entoncesthen / soParlas bien, entonces entiendo — You speak well, so I understand
Sentence Structure with Conjunctions

The standard order stays Subject + Verb + Object in both clauses. The conjunction simply connects them:

  • Parlo Nuviá porque amo la língua.I speak Nuviá because I love the language.
  • Si parlas lento, entiendo mejor.If you speak slowly, I understand better.
  • Volo andare, ma est tarde.I want to go, but it's late.

Chapter Four

Object Pronouns

Object pronouns replace a noun that receives the action of a verb. In Nuviá they are placed directly before the verb.

SubjectDirect ObjectMeaningExample
IumemeIla me vide — She sees me
TuteyouIu te amo — I love you
Illohim / it (masc.)Lo vide — (She/he) sees him
Ilalaher / it (fem.)La escucho — (I/he/she) listens to her
NunosusIlos nos viden — They see us
Voivosyou allIlu vos ama — He loves you all
Ilos / Ilaslos / lasthemLos conoco — (I) know them
Position Rule

Object pronouns go before the conjugated verb — always.

  • Iu te vido.I see you.
  • Ila no me escucha.She doesn't listen to me.
  • Ke los conoces?Do you know them?
  • Nos viden cada dia.They see us every day.
With Future (va + infinitive)

When using the future, the object pronoun still sits before va:

  • Te va chiamare.I will call you. (will-call you)
  • Los va vider mañana.She will see them tomorrow.
Object Pronouns in Use
Marco
Kai! Te va chiamare pos ceno.Kai! I will call you after dinner.
Kai
Vale! Nos va vider también — no me olvides!Okay! We'll see each other too — don't forget me!

Chapter Five

Progressive & Modal Verbs

The progressive tense describes something currently happening. Modal verbs express ability, obligation, or desire. Both expand expressive power dramatically.

❧

The Progressive — estare + gerund

Forming the Gerund

-are verbs: stem + -ando  ·  parlare → parlando
-ere verbs: stem + -endo  ·  videre → videndo

Progressive — Present

Iu *esto parlandoI am speaking
Tu *estas parlandoyou are speaking
Il/Ilaesta parlandohe/she is speaking
Nuestamos parlandowe are speaking
Voiestate parlandoyou all are speaking
Ilos/Ilasestan parlandothey are speaking

More Gerund Examples

mangiandoeating
videndoseeing
andandogoing
aprendandolearning
escribendowriting
leendoreading

* Iu and Tu may still be dropped — Esto parlando = I am speaking.

❧

Chapter Six

Comparatives & Superlatives

Nuviá expresses comparison with invariable particles placed before adjectives. The structure is simple and consistent across all words.

TypeStructureExample
More thanpiù + adj + deIl car est più rapido de il bico. The car is faster than the bike.
Less thanmenos + adj + deIl te est menos forte de il cafe. The tea is less strong than the coffee.
As … as(tanto) … comoLuna est tanto bela como Sofia. Luna is as beautiful as Sofia.
Superlativeil/la più + adjEst il più grande. It is the biggest.
Least of allil/la menos + adjEst la menos cara. It is the least expensive.
Irregular Comparatives

Two adjectives have irregular comparative forms used alongside (or instead of) the regular forms:

AdjectiveRegularIrregular (common)
bono (good)più bonomejor (better) · il mejor (best)
malo (bad)più malopeor (worse) · il peor (worst)
Comparison Examples
  • Il libro est più interesante de il film.The book is more interesting than the film.
  • Parlo mejor pos practicar.I speak better after practicing.
  • La casa est tanto grande como la escola.The house is as big as the school.
  • Est il peor cafe del mundo.It's the worst coffee in the world.

Pratica 6

Practice · Comparatives

  • Translate: She speaks better than me
  • Translate: This book is the most interesting
  • Translate: Coffee is as strong as tea
  • Translate: Today is the worst day
  1. Il mar est più belo de il ciel
  2. Ila parla mejor de me
  3. Questo libro est il più interesante
  4. Il cafe est tanto forte como il te

Colors & Descriptions

Colors in Nuviá follow the same adjective agreement rules from Book One — most agree in gender and number, though some are invariable.

rosso/rossared
blublue (inv.)
verdegreen (inv.)
gialo/gialayellow
bianco/biancawhite
negro/negrablack
narango/narangaorange
violapurple (inv.)
rosapink (inv.)
marronbrown (inv.)
griso/grisagray
dorado/doradagolden
Agreement in Practice

Invariable colors (blu, verde, viola, rosa, marron) never change form.
Variable colors add -a for feminine singular, -i for masc. plural, -e for fem. plural.

  • il car rossothe red car (masc. sg.)
  • la casa rossathe red house (fem. sg.)
  • i cari rossithe red cars (masc. pl.)
  • le case rossethe red houses (fem. pl.)
  • la porta bluthe blue door (invariable)
  • le porte bluthe blue doors (still blu)

Chapter Twelve

Extended Dialogues

This final chapter of Book Two brings everything together. Three extended dialogues — a café conversation, a trip to the market, and a farewell — use vocabulary and grammar from both books. Read aloud and pay attention to how the structures combine naturally.

❧

Dialogue 1 — Un Cafe con Amicos

A Coffee with Friends — Sunday Morning
Sofia
Bon dia, amicos! Ke volos beber? Habeo cafe e te.Good morning, friends! What do you want to drink? I have coffee and tea.
Marco
Volo cafe sin lecho, grátie. Esto muy stresado — devo trabajare oggi.I want coffee without milk, thanks. I'm very stressed — I have to work today.
Luna
Aunque est il donia, devas trabajare? Que tristesa!Even though it's Sunday, you have to work? How sad!
Marco
Sí, ma pos, volo mangiare la pasta más deliciosa del mundo con vos!Yes, but afterward, I want to eat the most delicious pasta in the world with you all!
Sofia
Entonces te va vider al restauranto a le oto de sera!Then we'll see you at the restaurant at 8 in the evening!
